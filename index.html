Why This Exists

Inspired by the Cockroach Janta Party (CJP) movement of 2026 — a symbol of India's youth refusing to be ignored. RoachWatch was built in that same spirit — not to spread anger, but to present verified, sourced facts about promises made, exams leaked, and where India truly stands globally.

Our Sources

Every card links to its sources, ranked by reliability: Primary (official documents) > Government (ministries, RBI, courts) > Academic (peer-reviewed research) > News (established publications). If a source is missing, we don't display the claim as fact.

Manifesto and campaign documents are used only to establish what was promised — never as proof of an outcome. Outcomes are verified against government data, court records, or audited reports.

🪳 What is RoachWatch?
RoachWatch is an independent, non-partisan accountability platform tracking government promises, exam paper leaks, and India's global standing — with verified sources on every claim.

The name is inspired by the Cockroach Janta Party (CJP) movement of 2026, when India's Chief Justice called unemployed youth "cockroaches." The youth didn't protest — they built. So did we.
📋 How We Classify Promises
❌ Broken — Promise made, clearly not delivered. Data from government or independent sources confirms failure.

💀 Done Badly — Promise technically fulfilled but implementation was so poor it caused harm, or the outcome was the opposite of intent.

⏳ Pending — Promise made, deadline passed or approaching, status unclear or incomplete.

✅ Kept — Promise made and delivered, verified by independent sources.
🔍 Our Source Standards
Every card on this platform links to at least one of the following:

GOV — Government Documents COURT — SC / HC Orders REPORT — Research Reports NEWS — Established Publications ACADEMIC — Peer Reviewed

We do not publish claims without at least one verifiable source. If a source link is missing, that card is marked for review.
⚖️ Legal & Corrections
All information published here is based on publicly available data. We rely on Supreme Court judgments, RBI publications, World Bank data, and established media — not opinions.

If you believe any data is incorrect, outdated, or misrepresented, use the feedback form in this menu. We take corrections seriously and will update promptly with a source note.

This platform does not target any individual. It tracks institutional outcomes and policy records.
